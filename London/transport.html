Getting Around

Sometimes getting around London can be tough. The main ways to get around are bus, underground, DLR, overground, trams and national rail.

Buses

Buses are the easiest and cheapest way to get around London. They run all over London and can easily help you get from Point A to Point B.

Underground

The underground is the metro system beneath London. There are lots of lines going through Central London.

National Rail

National Rail is the UK's rail system. Within London, it mainly goes through Central London and the London Suburbs. There are several train lines to take you long and short distances.

DLR (Docklands Light Railway)

The DLR is a light rail system running through eastern London. It is very fast and easy to get from place to place.

London Overground

The Overground is a rail system that runs in South and North London.

London Trams

The tram is a quick connection between South-East and South-West London.

Airports

The main airports in London are Heathrow Airport, Gatwick Airport and Stanstead Airport.

Heathrow Airport

To get here you can: take the Heathrow Express from London Paddington, costing £15; take TfL Rail from London Paddington; or take the Piccadilly Line from Central London.

Gatwick Airport

To get here you can: take the Gatwick Express from London Victoria, costing £20; or take National Rail (Southern) from London Victoria.

Stanstead Airport

To get here you can: take the Stanstead Express from London Liverpool Street, costing £15; or take National Rail (Greater Anglia) from London Liverpool Street as well.

Train Stations

The main train stations are London Kings Cross/London St Pancras, London Victoria, London Paddington, and London Bridge.

London Kings Cross/London St Pancras International

From London Kings Cross and London St Pancras International, the trains mainly go North. From St Pancras, there are trains to France, Belgium and Amsterdam. Also, there are trains to the South-East of the UK.

London Victoria

From London Victoria, the trains go South towards Brighton and also South-East towards Dover.

London Paddington

From London Paddington, the trains go West towards Wales and Bristol.

London Bridge

From London Bridge, the trains go towards the South-East.
