Getting Around

Sometimes getting around London can be tough. If you need a tube map to help you get around go to this link, or use this pdf.

General Travel

The main ways to get around are by bus, Underground, National Rail, DLR, Overground, and Trams. Also, dogs are allowed on all TfL services.

Buses
Buses are the easiest and cheapest way to get around London. They run all over London and can easily help you get from Point A to Point B.
Underground
The underground is the metro system beneath London. There are lots of lines going through Central London.
National Rail
National Rail is the UK's rail system. Within London, it mainly goes through Central London and the London Suburbs. There are several train lines to take you long and short distances.
DLR
The DLR is a light rail system running through eastern London. It is very fast and easy to get from place to place.
London Overground
The Overground is a rail system that runs in South and North London.
London Trams
The tram is a quick connection between south east and south west London.
Elizabeth Line
The Elizabeth Line opened earlier this year, it cuts across Central London very quickly. It will be fully open on the 23rd of October.

Airports

The main airports in London are Heathrow Airport, Gatwick Airport, London City Airport, Stanstead Airport, Southend Airport, and Luton Airport.

Heathrow Airport
To get here you can: take the Heathrow Express from London Paddington, costing £15; take the Elizabeth Line from London Paddington; or take the Piccadilly Line from Central London.
Gatwick Airport
To get here you can: take the Gatwick Express from London Victoria, costing £20; take National Rail (Southern) from London Victoria; take National Rail(Thameslink) from London Blackfriars, or London Bridge.
Stanstead Airport
To get here you can: take the Stanstead Express from London Liverpool Street, costing £15; or take National Rail (Greater Anglia) from London Liverpool Street as well.
London City Airport
To get here you can: take the DLR from one of the many stations within London.
London Southend Airport
To get here you can: take National Rail (Greater Anglia) from London Liverpool Street.
London Luton Airport
To get here you can: take National Rail (Thameslink) from London St Pancras International, London Bridge, or London Bridge; take National Rail (East Midlands Railway) from London St Pancras International.

Train Stations

The main train stations are London St Pancras International, London Victoria, London Paddington, London Liverpool Street, London Bridge, London Blackfriars. All of these stations have national rail services and underground services. Also, dogs are allowed on all National Rail services.

London St Pancras International
From London St Pancras International, the trains go north towards Luton and Nottingham, as well as to the south east towards Dover. There are also trains to France, Belgium, and the Netherlands. Located at Euston Rd, London N1C 4QP. Nearby, in Coal Drops Yard, there are lots of places to eat and drink.
London Victoria
From London Victoria, the trains go South towards Brighton and also south east towards Dover. Located at Victoria St, London SW1E 5ND.
London Paddington
From London Paddington, the trains go West towards Wales and Bristol. Located at Praed St, London W2 1HB.
London Liverpool Street
From London Liverpool Street, the trains go North East towards Ipswich. Located at Liverpool St, London EC2M 7PY.
London Bridge
From London Bridge, the trains go South East towards Dover. Located at 21 Duke St Hill, London SE1 2SW.
London Blackfriars
From London Blackfriars, the trains go South East towards Sevenoaks. Located at 179 Queen Victoria St, London EC4V 4DY.
